What this is

Most scaling software companies don't struggle because the people are wrong.

They struggle because a specific pattern has taken hold — something small that's quietly shaping everything else, and that nobody has quite named yet.

The Delivery Pattern Diagnostic is a short, direct assessment for CEOs of scaling software companies. It takes about ten minutes. You answer questions about what's actually happening in your team — not how you'd describe it at a conference, but what you see day to day. At the end, it names the pattern most likely at work in your organisation, how it typically plays out, and three things you can do about it this week.

The patterns come from ten years of watching delivery break in scaling software companies — first-customer rollouts, companies finding their way after finding product-market fit, leadership stretched thin by competing priorities creating a vacuum the team has to navigate around. They're not a framework. They're the specific ways scaling delivery actually breaks.
